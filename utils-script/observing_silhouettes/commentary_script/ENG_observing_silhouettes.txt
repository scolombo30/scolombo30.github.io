Observing Silhouettes
By removing facial features, individuals are transformed into archetypes. They are no longer specific people, but a representation of universal humanity. In these shots, we stand exactly behind them, creating an 'observation of the observer' effect. A silent bond is formed between the photographer, the subject, and the viewer.

In 'The Infinite Detour', the grainy black and white and the presence of the bicycle suggest a pause during a nocturnal exploration. A cinematic narrative.

In 'Twin Stars, Silent Hearts', the energy becomes mystical. The two spheres appear as deities watched by the couple at the end of time, floating in deep darkness. 'Observing Silhouettes' suggests that, in this moment, the pure act of vision is the only thing that truly matters.
